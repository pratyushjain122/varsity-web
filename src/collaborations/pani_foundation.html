Rainwater harvesting and watershed management project

DATE : 1 May
EVENT : Rainwater harvesting and watershed management Project
ORGANIZATION: Paani Foundation

The world is heating up. Red alert is being sounded in most of the parts of India. Rural areas of Maharashtra have been facing droughts. For decades, drought has severely affected thousands of villages in the state. It is a crisis largely caused by humans- by poor management of water and rapid destruction of nature.

Hence, on Maharashtra Day, i.e. May 1st, V@rSITy Care joined the Maharashramdaan, a rainwater harvesting and watershed management project arranged by the Paani Foundation, to make Maharashtra drought free. This foundation is an NGO set up by the team of Satyamev Jayate with the mission of making Maharashtra drought-free and prosperous. Garade, village near Saswad, about 60km from Pune, took part in this.

The students of SIT signed up to volunteer for this noble initiative and participated in Mahashramdaan. The volunteers were taken on a tour in the village to show the different water conservation techniques which they have taken up. The village firstly made sure that everyone is united for the sake of the basic requirement, water, cutting across all socio-economic barriers. They, then underwent training on rainwater harvesting by Paani Foundation and started working. All the reservoirs and canals were desilted and their depth was increased. This visit gave the volunteers, a deeper and practical insight into rainwater harvesting and the water crisis that we are facing.
