Webinar 1 : Dealing with Uncertainties

DATE: 13 June, 2020

ORGANIZATION: ABHYUDAY , The Social body of IIT Bombay

LOCATION: Virtual

On 13th June 2020, a webinar on MENTAL HEALTH-“Dealing With Uncertainties’’ was held to create awareness and positivity among people during the tough time everyone is going through. Instagram, Facebook and Whatsapp were used as an online platform to inform people about the webinar. We collaborated for the same with the drama club of college –M.O.S.A.I.C and ABHYUDAY ,The Social body of IIT Bombay. Various promotional events were planned for the same. First one was the doodle-art competition ‘DOODLIE-DOO’ in which the participants were asked to draw doodles on the topic- Moods, Mind and Mental Health and were featured on the club’s Instagram page. Around 30 people participated in it and the winners were declared after the webinar. A video, prepared by M.O.S.A.I.C, the drama club of SIT, was shared on Instagram on how mental is being affected during the lockdown. Another event was- any phase in life which they want to talk about and last was the one in which ten simple questions with options ‘YES’ or ‘NO’ were asked on the club’s page. Around 90-100 peoples joined on the day of webinar. The webinar included three panelists who discussed about various issues and how to tackle all the stressful thoughts- Dr. Anuradha Harkare, Founder, Mind Catalyst Study Counselling. She talked about how right now people’s minds are full of various uncertainties and the importance to deal with it. She further discussed how difficult it is for students to adapt to a complete new process of learning, how pattern of exams has changed, and how this all has affected final year students the most. Ms. Anushree Lokhande, Founder, Evolving together. She discussed about how one need to manage emotions and thoughts. Emotions become intense as one try to ignore them, bury them or misidentify them. The topics that were covered were motional awareness, management of emotions, link between thoughts and emotions, mood boosters, etc. Dr. Girija Mahale, Head, Symbiosis Centre of Emotional Well Being. She talked about how people need to convert challenges into opportunities and how one need to think out of the box and try things not done before. She also talked about self care and other things like- physical activity, sleep hygiene, etc. Ms. Mayuri Dutta was the guest speaker and she talked about mindfulness, take things as it comes to us. She further explained how every problem is an opportunity and the need to take care in the present scenario. At the end of the webinar a question and answer session was held where the participants got a chance to interact and discuss their issues with the three exceptional panelists The webinar made everyone understand that even though the life is full of uncertainties, important thing is that one must look for positive aspects and adapt to the changes to lead a stress free life.

Webinar 2 : Combating the Enemy within Depression

DATE: 8 August, 2020

ORGANIZATION: ABHYUDAY , The Social body of IIT Bombay

LOCATION: Virtual

On 8th August 2020, a webinar on COMBATING THE ENEMY WITHIN: DEPRESSION was held, by V@rsity Care in collaboration with Abhyuday, The Social body of IIT Bombay, to create awareness among people. Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook and Whatsapp were used as an online platform to inform people about the webinar. To reach out to more people we contacted several promotional websites like Scoopit, Digg, Webinarkhoj and instagram pages that already speaks about such topic like mentalhealthsupport.__, toyourlife and admirebrainy.

Various promotional events were planned for the same.First one was the writing competition for which the theme was ‘Lost in Myself, Moods and Depression’. The participants were asked to submit their work directly to the club’s instagram handle and those write-ups were anonymously uploaded for voting. The one with maximum votes was chosen as winner. A dance video, depicting internal feelings and moods, prepared by members of the club, was shared on Instagram. Another event was “MUSIC FOR THE SOUL” in which there was a live performance on the club’s instagram handle with KALA JOWSH, Musical Band of SIT. A short movie was made to depict that how there is sudden disinterest and loss of energy in one’s life because of depression. Around 85 people attended the webinar including three panelists who talked about what is depression, how to tackle it etc. The webinar included three panelists who discussed about various issues and how to tackle all the stressful thoughts-

Our first Panelist, Dr Girija Mahale, Head, Symbiosis Centre For Emotional Well being ,mainly talked about how sadness and depression are two different things. She explained that how everyone is sad but not everyone is depressed. She talked about various symptoms like inability to enjoy things, difficult to concentrate, etc. and possible causes of depression in different age group of people.

Dr. Anushree Lokhande, Founder, Evolving Together spoke about how to be a good support and how to get help. She explained the three types of boundaries- porous, healthy and rigid. One needs to manage emotions and practice mindfulness to be happy. She also explained whom and when to approach someone for help.

Dr. Anurag, HOD of Psychiatry, MIMS Hyderabad talked about stigma around mental health issues and the need of proper medication. He explained that how 1 in 6 people suffer from neuropsychiatric disease but many don’t come forward to talk about it because of society which often results in them committing suicides. He explained various steps to prevent suicide such as establish communication, seek professional help and listen more than giving advice.


At the end of the session there was a Q&A session held where the people got a chance to interact and ask their questions to the three exceptional panelists. The webinar made everyone understand that rather than being judgmental, people should be more compassionate towards the one who are facing such problems so that they can talk more openly about it without being afraid of any stigma.
